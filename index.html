Introduction to Literature

  Literature is a collection of written works that express thoughts, feelings, and attitudes towards life. It serves as a permanent expression of these thoughts and emotions, offering insights into life and the world

  Beyond mere documentation, literature becomes an imaginative work that provides readers with recreation, recognition, revelation, and redemption. It is a realm where creativity and language intertwine, allowing readers to explore and find deeper meaning in human experiences

Characteristics of Literature

  • Literature is imaginative
  • Literature expresses thoughts and feelings
  • Literature deals with life experience
  • Literature uses words in a powerful, effective and yet captivating manner
  • Literature promotes recreation and revelation of hidden facts.

Forms of Literature

  • Spoken Literature - literature that's handed down from generation to generation by word of mouth.
  • Written Literature - hand written, recorded or printed.

Pre-Colonial Period Literature

  Pre-colonial Philippine literature was primarily oral, with rich traditions of epics, folk tales, myths, riddles, and proverbs transmitted through generations. Epics like "Biag ni Lam-ang" were chanted, while riddles and proverbs conveyed wisdom. Songs, poems, and aguinaldos were integral to indigenous communities. The oral tradition played a crucial role in preserving cultural heritage.

Pre-Colonial Period Writing System

  Before the introduction of the Latin alphabet brought by the Spaniards, Filipino ancestors had their own alphabet. The first alphabet used by our ancestors was similar to that of the Malayo-Polynesian alphabet. This includes Kulita, Kurdita, Haninu'o, Baybayin, Buhid, Basahan, Tagbanwa, Badlit, Jawi, and Krim.


  Baybayin is an ancient script used primarily by the Tagalog people. Baybayin means to spell, write, and syllabize.
